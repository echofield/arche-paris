# Aura ↔ Accomplishments, Carte ↔ Achievements

**Purpose:** Design reference for future work. How the aura (glyph/companion) links to what’s been accomplished, and how the carte (card) extends through certain achievements ("put the thing farther for them").

## Aura and accomplishments

- **Aura (glyph + companion "Ember"):** Represents the boundary between the lived world and optional sealing / relation to the Field. It is not a score or wallet.
- **Accomplishments:** Quest closes, inscriptions written, manual walks added. These already bump the companion level (0–3) and can be sealed optionally. The aura does not "display" accomplishments as a list; it reacts subtly (one word: Quiet / Awake / Warm / Bright).
- **Link:** The aura is the single sanctioned entry point for optional sealing. Accomplishments are sealed only when the user chooses, via the glyph. The aura should never become a dashboard of achievements; it stays a boundary symbol.

## Carte and achievements

- **Carte (card):** The user’s physical or digital card (e.g. PS-XXXX). It identifies them to the world layer and to the kernel (when used).
- **Achievements:** Quest completions, traces, walks, inscriptions. These are lived experience; they are not "unlocks" that gate content. Achievements may later inform what can be sealed or how the carte is presented (e.g. "your trace in the city"), without turning the carte into a badge board.
- **Put the thing farther:** The carte could deepen or extend through use — not by levels or rewards, but by the accumulation of traces, sealed moments, and presence. The carte remains one card; its meaning grows with what the user has done, in a way that stays calm and optional.

## Principles

- No gamification: no XP, no ranks, no "achievement unlocked" UI.
- Sealing is optional and user-initiated.
- The aura stays minimal (one word, one glyph); the carte stays one card.
- Affiliation and economics remain outside the Fade; they do not alter meaning inside the world.
